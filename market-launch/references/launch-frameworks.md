# Launch Frameworks Reference

## Complete Task Lists by Phase

### Weeks 1-2: Foundation

**Positioning and Messaging:**
- [ ] Write a single positioning statement: "[Product] is for [audience] who want [outcome] without [pain point]. Unlike [alternative], we [key differentiator]."
- [ ] Define the primary value proposition (one sentence a non-expert would understand)
- [ ] Identify the top 3 objections and draft responses

**Landing Page:**
- [ ] Headline: lead with the outcome, not the feature. "Get X without Y" or "Finally, Z that actually works"
- [ ] Subheadline: who it's for and what makes it different
- [ ] 3-5 bullet points: benefits (not features) with specificity
- [ ] Social proof section: logos, testimonials, number of users, media mentions
- [ ] Primary CTA: one action, clear label (not "Submit" — "Get Early Access", "Start Free Trial")
- [ ] Email capture with incentive (early bird discount, exclusive bonus, or waitlist)

**Analytics Setup:**
- [ ] Install Google Analytics / Plausible / Fathom
- [ ] Set up conversion tracking on the primary CTA
- [ ] Add UTM parameters to all launch links
- [ ] Create a simple dashboard for live monitoring during launch week

**Email and Content:**
- [ ] Import email list to ESP (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, Klaviyo)
- [ ] Draft all 7 email subjects and preview text
- [ ] Write Email 1 (Teaser) and Email 2 (Reveal) — full body copy

**Beta:**
- [ ] Identify 10-20 target beta users (existing customers, engaged audience, or manual outreach)
- [ ] Prepare beta invitation message
- [ ] Set up feedback collection (Typeform, Airtable, or simple email thread)

---

### Weeks 3-4: Audience Building

**Content Seeding:**
- [ ] Publish 2-3 pieces of content related to the problem your product solves (not promoting the product yet — building the authority)
- [ ] Post 1 thread per week on Twitter/X teasing the problem space
- [ ] Share 1 LinkedIn post per week with a relevant insight

**Community Engagement:**
- [ ] Identify 3-5 communities where the target audience lives (subreddits, Facebook groups, Slack communities, Discord servers)
- [ ] Participate authentically in those communities — answer questions, add value
- [ ] Do not promote the product yet. Build presence first.

**Beta Onboarding:**
- [ ] Onboard first 5-10 beta users
- [ ] Conduct 2-3 user interviews (30 minutes each) — what they like, what's confusing, what's missing
- [ ] Collect 3-5 beta testimonials with specific results and numbers

**Partner Outreach:**
- [ ] Identify 5-10 potential partners (complementary products, newsletters, influencers)
- [ ] Send initial contact emails — not a pitch, a genuine connection and curiosity about collaboration
- [ ] Offer free access to the product in exchange for feedback and potential promotion

---

### Weeks 5-6: Pre-Launch Intensification

**Email Sequence:**
- [ ] Write Email 3 (Social Proof), Email 4 (Launch), Email 5 (Follow-Up), Email 6 (Objections), Email 7 (Urgency)
- [ ] Set up email automation in ESP — schedule all 7 emails in sequence
- [ ] Test every email on mobile before scheduling
- [ ] Send a test email to yourself and 2-3 colleagues — check formatting, links, and CTA

**Launch Assets:**
- [ ] Create 3-5 social graphics (for the launch post and the thread)
- [ ] Write all social media posts for launch week (see Social Media section below)
- [ ] Record a 60-90 second product demo video (optional but strongly recommended)
- [ ] Create the Product Hunt assets if applicable (tagline, gallery images, hunter outreach)

**Partner Briefing:**
- [ ] Send swipe copy to all confirmed partners: email, social, blog, and a unique affiliate/referral link
- [ ] Brief each partner on the launch date, time, and what you're asking of them specifically
- [ ] Confirm participation from each partner — get a "yes, I'll post at [time]" in writing

**Urgency Mechanism:**
- [ ] Set an early bird pricing structure (15-30% discount that expires at a specific date/time)
- [ ] Or: Define a limited quantity (first 50 customers get X bonus)
- [ ] Or: Expiring bonus (free onboarding call, exclusive workshop, bonus content — until [date])
- [ ] This mechanism must be communicated in Email 4, Email 5, and Email 7

---

### Week 7: Launch Week — Day by Day

**Monday (Soft Launch):**
- [ ] Send Email 4 (The Launch) at 9-10 AM in the audience's primary timezone
- [ ] Post soft launch announcement on social: "We're live. Here's what we built and why..."
- [ ] Notify beta users personally: "You're getting first access before the public"
- [ ] Monitor conversions, traffic, and email open rates — set alerts

**Tuesday (Public Announcement):**
- [ ] Publish the full launch thread on Twitter/X
- [ ] Publish LinkedIn launch post
- [ ] Submit to Product Hunt if that's a channel (launch at 12:01 AM PT for best results)
- [ ] Post in communities (authentic, non-spammy — "I've been building in public here, today's the day")
- [ ] Ask beta users and close network to share, upvote, or comment

**Wednesday (Social Proof Day):**
- [ ] Send Email 5 (Social Proof Follow-Up)
- [ ] Share the first customer results on social: "24 hours in. Here's what's happening..."
- [ ] Post a behind-the-scenes update: reactions, number of customers, milestone if hit
- [ ] Reply to every comment and DM within 2 hours

**Thursday (Objection Handling):**
- [ ] Send Email 6 (Objection Handler)
- [ ] Post a "Q&A — your top questions answered" on social
- [ ] If running paid ads: increase budget by 20-30% on top-performing ad sets

**Friday-Sunday (Urgency Close):**
- [ ] Send Email 7 (Urgency Close) on Friday
- [ ] Post "48 hours left" on all social channels
- [ ] Send a final "closing tonight" reminder the last evening
- [ ] Turn off urgency mechanism at the stated deadline — maintain trust

---

### Week 8: Post-Launch

**Retrospective:**
- [ ] Pull final metrics: total revenue, conversion rate, email performance, best traffic source
- [ ] Document what worked and what didn't — be specific
- [ ] Send a "thank you and what's next" email to all customers and subscribers

**Customer Onboarding:**
- [ ] Confirm every new customer received their welcome email and access
- [ ] Send a personal email or video from the founder to the first 20 customers
- [ ] Set up a 7-day onboarding sequence if not already done

**Ongoing Engine:**
- [ ] Turn the launch content into evergreen content (best-performing thread → blog post)
- [ ] Set up a referral program for existing customers
- [ ] Plan the next launch or major product update
- [ ] Define the ongoing marketing calendar based on launch learnings

## Email Templates

### Email 1 — The Teaser (2 Weeks Before Launch)

```
Subject: Something we've been building for [audience type]...

Hey [first name],

For the past [X months], I've been working on something I'm really excited to share with you.

I'm not ready to reveal everything yet — but I wanted to give you a heads up because you'll be one of the first to hear about it.

Here's what I can tell you:

It's built for [who it's for].

It solves [the core problem] — which I know has been a frustration for a lot of you based on the questions I get.

And it works differently from anything I've seen out there because [brief differentiator — one sentence].

More details coming [date]. Keep an eye on your inbox.

Talk soon,
[Name]

P.S. — [First name], if this sounds like something you've been waiting for, reply to this email and let me know. I'm reserving early access for people who are genuinely interested.
```

### Email 2 — The Reveal (1 Week Before Launch)

```
Subject: Here it is: [Product Name] launches [date]

Hey [first name],

I promised you something. Here it is.

[Product Name] is [what it is in one sentence]. It's for [who], and it helps you [outcome].

[Screenshot or GIF of the product in action]

Here's what beta users are saying:

"[Specific testimonial with real numbers — e.g., 'I went from 200 to 1,200 monthly visitors in 6 weeks']" — [Name, Title]

We launch on [date] at [time].

Here's what you need to know:
- Early bird pricing: [price] (goes up to [regular price] after [date])
- What's included: [brief list]
- Who it's for: [specific audience description]

I'll send you the link the moment we're live.

[Name]

P.S. — Questions? Hit reply. I'm reading every one.
```

### Email 3 — Social Proof (3 Days Before Launch)

```
Subject: What beta users are saying (3 days to go)

Hey [first name],

Three days until [Product Name] launches.

Before we go live, I wanted to share what our beta users have experienced:

---

[Beta user 1 name], [their role/title]:
"[Specific quote with a measurable result — e.g., 'Saved 8 hours per week and cut our ad spend by 40%']"

[Beta user 2 name]:
"[Another specific quote]"

[Beta user 3 name]:
"[Third quote]"

---

These are real people getting real results.

Launch is [date] at [time]. Early bird pricing of [price] is available for the first [X] customers / through [date].

[Name]
```

### Email 4 — The Launch (Day 1)

```
Subject: We're live. [Product Name] is officially open.

Hey [first name],

Today's the day.

[Product Name] is officially open for enrollment.

[Clear description of what it is in 2-3 sentences]

What you get:
- [Deliverable 1]
- [Deliverable 2]
- [Deliverable 3]

Early bird pricing: [price] (this offer closes [date/time])

[Big CTA Button: Get [Product Name] →]

[URL]

Questions? Reply to this email. I read every one.

[Name]

P.S. — [Reminder of the urgency mechanism: "Early bird pricing closes in 72 hours. After that, the price increases to [regular price]."]
```

### Email 5 — Social Proof Follow-Up (Day 3)

```
Subject: 48 hours in. Here's what's happening.

Hey [first name],

[Product Name] has been open for 48 hours.

[Share a milestone: X customers, X revenue, a specific moment that happened]

Here's what the first customers are saying:

"[Fresh quote from a paying customer if possible, or a beta user]"

And a few things I want to address:

[Common question 1]: [Answer]
[Common question 2]: [Answer]

[Product Name] closes early bird pricing at [date/time]. After that, the price goes up to [regular price].

[CTA: Join [Product Name] →]

[Name]
```

### Email 6 — Objection Handler (Day 4)

```
Subject: "Is [Product Name] right for me?" — honest answers

Hey [first name],

I know some of you are still on the fence. Let me address the questions I've been getting.

"I'm not sure I have time for this."
[Answer — e.g., "This takes [X] minutes per day. Most customers spend less time on this than they do checking email."]

"I've tried [competing approach] before and it didn't work."
[Answer — e.g., "Here's what's different about [Product Name]: [specific differentiator]. The reason [competing approach] doesn't work is [reason]."]

"I'm not sure it will work for my [specific situation]."
[Answer — e.g., "It works specifically for [type of person] because [reason]. If you're [exception], it's probably not the right fit."]

"What if it doesn't work for me?"
[Guarantee or risk reversal: "We offer a [X]-day money-back guarantee. If you don't see [outcome], I'll refund you in full. No questions asked."]

Still have questions? Reply to this email. I answer personally.

[CTA: Get [Product Name] →]
Early bird pricing closes [date/time].

[Name]
```

### Email 7 — Urgency Close (Day 5-7)

```
Subject: Closing tonight: [Product Name]

Hey [first name],

This is the last email I'll send about [Product Name].

Tonight at [time], early bird pricing closes. [Or: enrollment closes / the bonus expires.]

After that, [what changes — e.g., "the price goes from $X to $Y" / "enrollment closes until [date]"].

Here's everything one more time:

[Product Name] is [what it is].

It helps [who] achieve [outcome] without [pain point].

[What's included — brief list]

Price: [price] until tonight at [time]

[CTA: Get [Product Name] — Last Chance →]

[URL]

If [Product Name] isn't right for you, no worries. I'll be back with other things.

But if you've been thinking about it — tonight's the night.

[Name]
```

## Social Media Templates

### Twitter/X Launch Thread

```
Tweet 1 (Hook):
[Product Name] is officially live.

Here's what we built, why it took [X months], and why I think it changes how [audience] do [activity]. 🧵

Tweet 2 (Problem):
[Audience type] spend [X hours] every [week/month] on [pain point].

Most of them get [bad result].

It's not because they're doing it wrong. It's because [root cause].

Tweet 3 (Story):
I spent [X years] dealing with this personally.

I tried [solution 1]. [What happened].
I tried [solution 2]. [What happened].

Nothing worked until I figured out [key insight].

Tweet 4 (Solution):
[Product Name] is built on that insight.

It [how it works in one sentence].

Instead of [old approach], you [new approach].

Tweet 5 (Results):
Beta users have already seen:
- [Specific result with numbers]
- [Specific result with numbers]
- [Specific result with numbers]

[Optional: screenshot of beta result]

Tweet 6 (CTA):
[Product Name] is live today.

Early bird pricing: [price]

This closes [date/time].

[URL]

Tweet 7 (P.S.):
Questions? Reply to this thread. I'm here all day.
```

### LinkedIn Launch Post

```
[Attention-grabbing first line — something personal, a counterintuitive claim, or a compelling statistic. Do not start with "I'm excited to announce"]

[2-3 sentences about the problem — make it personal and relatable to the reader's experience]

[What you built and why — lead with the transformation, not the features]

After [X months] of building [privately / with a small group of beta users], [Product Name] is live today.

Here's what it does: [One sentence]

Here's who it's for: [One sentence]

Here's what beta users experienced: [2-3 brief results]

[Price and timing: "Early bird pricing through [date] — link in first comment."]

If you know someone who [has the problem], tag them below. This could be exactly what they need.
```

## Metrics Benchmarks by Launch Type

| Metric | Email-First Launch | Social-First Launch | Product Hunt | Paid Ads Launch |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Email open rate (Day 1) | 35-55% | 25-40% | 30-45% | 20-35% |
| Email click rate (Day 1) | 8-15% | 5-10% | 6-12% | 5-8% |
| Landing page conversion (warm list) | 10-30% | 5-15% | 5-20% | 2-8% |
| Landing page conversion (cold) | 2-5% | 1-4% | 3-8% | 1-4% |
| Revenue from Day 1 | 25-40% of total | 30-50% | 40-60% | 50-70% |
| Revenue from follow-up emails | 60-75% of total | 50-70% | 40-60% | 30-50% |

**Note:** These are reference ranges, not guarantees. List quality, product-market fit, price point, and execution quality are the primary determinants.
