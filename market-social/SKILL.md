# Social Media Content Calendar & Generation

Generate a complete 30-day content calendar with platform-specific posts, hooks, hashtags, and a content repurposing strategy. Every post is ready to publish or hand to a social media manager.

## Invocation

Run as `/market social <topic/url>`. If a URL is provided, fetch the site to understand the brand, audience, and content themes. If a topic is provided, build the strategy around that topic. Output the full calendar to `SOCIAL-CALENDAR.md`.

---

## Phase 1: Brand and Audience Discovery

### 1.1 Brand Context

Establish before generating any content — posts that don't match brand voice signal inauthenticity and damage trust:

| Context Element | Source | Purpose |
|----------------|--------|---------|
| Brand name | URL or user input | Consistent branding across all posts |
| Industry | Site analysis | Industry-relevant topics and terminology |
| Target audience | About page, copy, user input | Shapes language, pain points, and references |
| Brand voice | Existing social/site copy | Posts must match the tone the audience expects |
| Key products/services | Product/pricing pages | Drives promotional and proof-based content |
| Unique selling points | Homepage, feature pages | Differentiation angles for content |
| Competitors | Industry analysis | Gaps and opportunities for positioning content |

### 1.2 Platform Selection

Select 2-3 primary platforms and concentrate the calendar there. Spreading across all platforms leads to thin, inconsistent presence. Platform specs and content type mixes for each platform are in `references/social-platform-specs.md`.

Choose based on business type and audience:
- **LinkedIn**: B2B, SaaS, agencies, consultants — decision makers 25-54
- **Twitter/X**: Tech, media, real-time commentary — founders and tech-savvy audiences
- **Instagram**: E-commerce, lifestyle, creators — visual buyers 18-40
- **TikTok**: Consumer brands, education, creators — Gen Z and millennials
- **YouTube**: Long-form education, SaaS demos — research-intent audiences
- **Facebook**: Local business, communities, older demographics

## Phase 2: Content Strategy Framework

### 2.1 Content Pillars

Define 4-5 pillars that anchor all social content. Pillars ensure variety, prevent content fatigue, and build a recognizable brand identity over time. Content with no pillar structure tends to be random and fails to build a loyal following.

| Pillar | Type | Purpose | % of Content |
|--------|------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Educational | Establish authority; provide standalone value | 40% |
| 2 | Behind-the-Scenes | Build trust; humanize the brand | 20% |
| 3 | Social Proof | Build credibility; drive consideration | 15% |
| 4 | Engagement | Build community; signal to algorithm | 15% |
| 5 | Promotional | Drive revenue; announce offers | 10% |

The 40-10 educational-to-promotional ratio is critical. Audiences follow accounts that teach them things. They tolerate promotional content when it's rare and follows genuine value delivery. Brands that flip this ratio see steady engagement decline.

### 2.2 Content Types by Platform

Detailed content type mixes and technical specifications for each platform are in `references/social-platform-specs.md`. Key principle: use the content formats each platform's algorithm rewards, not the same format across every platform.

## Phase 3: Hooks and Opening Lines

The first line of a post (or first 3 seconds of a video) determines whether someone reads or scrolls past. Hooks are not optional — they are the single highest-leverage element in social content.

Full hook formula libraries for LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, and TikTok are in `references/social-platform-specs.md`.

Core hook categories to draw from:
- **Curiosity gap**: Tease the answer without giving it
- **Contrarian**: Challenge a widely-held belief
- **Specific result**: Lead with a concrete, impressive outcome
- **Audience call-out**: Directly name the specific person this is for
- **Story setup**: Open with a scene or moment that creates tension
- **Number promise**: "7 things / 3 mistakes / 5 ways"

Write at least 2 hook variants for each anchor post and select the stronger one.

## Phase 4: Hashtag Strategy

Use a tiered approach for every post. The goal is a mix of highly targeted tags (where you can rank) and broader tags (for discovery potential).

| Tier | Post Count Range | Count to Use | Purpose |
|------|-----------------|-------------|---------|
| Niche | Under 100K posts | 3-5 | Targeted; easier to rank and be discovered by the right audience |
| Mid-size | 100K–1M posts | 3-5 | Moderate competition; engaged, relevant audience |
| Broad | 1M+ posts | 2-3 | Discovery potential; lower per-post engagement rate |
| Branded | Custom | 1 | Brand recognition; UGC collection |

Platform-specific hashtag counts and research process are in `references/social-platform-specs.md`.

For each content pillar, build and document a hashtag set:
- 5 niche hashtags for the brand's sub-industry
- 5 mid-size hashtags for the broader industry
- 3 broad hashtags for general discovery
- 1 branded hashtag

## Phase 5: Content Repurposing Strategy

### 5.1 The 1-to-10 Framework

Content repurposing is the core efficiency principle of sustainable social media. Creating from scratch for every platform is the most common reason content programs burn out. Instead, create one high-quality anchor piece and extract 10+ derivative posts from it.

**Why this matters strategically:** Each piece of research, thinking, or storytelling has more value than a single post can express. Repurposing ensures the insights reach different audiences, on different platforms, in different formats — compounding the return on each content creation session. It also produces message consistency: all posts from the same anchor reinforce the same core idea.

```
SOURCE (Anchor Content):
  Blog post / Podcast episode / YouTube video / Newsletter / Webinar

DERIVATIVES:
  1.  LinkedIn text post — Lead insight or contrarian take
  2.  Twitter thread — 5-7 key takeaways, numbered
  3.  Instagram carousel — Main framework visualized as slides
  4.  Instagram Reel — 30-second summary of the #1 point
  5.  TikTok — Quick-tip format of the most surprising finding
  6.  LinkedIn carousel — PDF slideshow of the full framework
  7.  Twitter single tweet — The single most quotable line
  8.  Instagram Story — Behind-the-scenes of creating the piece
  9.  YouTube Short — 60-second condensed video version
  10. Facebook post — Discussion question based on the topic
  11. Quote graphic — Branded pull-quote image
  12. Email section — Adapted into a nurture email (links to /market emails)
```

### 5.2 Repurposing Schedule

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 1 | Publish the original anchor content |
| Day 1-2 | Share key insight on LinkedIn + Tweet thread |
| Day 3 | Instagram carousel + Reel from the framework |
| Day 5 | TikTok + YouTube Short |
| Day 7 | Different angle or secondary takeaway post |
| Day 10 | Engagement question related to the topic |
| Day 14 | "In case you missed it" reshare with new framing |

Include a repurposing plan for at least 2 anchor pieces in the 30-day calendar.

## Phase 6: Engagement Tactics

Include engagement-driving elements throughout the calendar:

**Questions (end 30% of posts with one):**
- "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]? Drop it below."
- "Agree or disagree? [Statement]"
- "Which one are you? A) [option] B) [option] C) [option]"

**Polls (1-2x per week on platforms that support them):**
- "What matters most to you in [category]?"
- "How often do you [behavior]?"
- "Which would you choose: [A] or [B]?"

**Debate/Contrarian Posts (1-2x per week — highest engagement driver):**
- "[Common advice] is terrible advice. Here's why..."
- "[Industry practice] is dead. Change my mind."
- "The industry won't tell you this, but [honest truth]."

**Storytelling Posts (1-2x per week):**
- "3 years ago, I [starting point]. Today, [result]. Here's the journey:"
- "The worst [professional situation] I ever had taught me [lesson]."

## Phase 7: 30-Day Content Calendar

### 7.1 Calendar Format

Generate a complete 30-day calendar with this structure for each day:

```
DAY 1 (Monday):
  [Platform 1]: [Pillar - Type]
    Hook: "[Opening line]"
    Post: [Full post text — ready to copy-paste]
    Visual: [Description of image, video, or carousel content needed]
    Hashtags: #tag1 #tag2 #tag3
    Time: [recommended send time]
    Type: [text post / carousel / reel / thread / etc.]

  [Platform 2]: [Pillar - Type]
    [Same structure]
```

### 7.2 Calendar Distribution Rules

Enforce these distribution rules across the 30 days:
- Each content pillar appears at least 6 times across the month
- Promotional content never appears on two consecutive days
- Engagement posts are spread evenly (every 2-3 days)
- Platform-specific formats maximize each platform's algorithm preferences
- Mix of content types — avoid runs of the same format
- Leave 4-6 "trend response" slots with placeholder guidance on how to adapt trending formats when they emerge

### 7.3 Weekly Themes

Organize the calendar into 4 weekly themes that create coherent progression:
- Week 1: Awareness / problem identification
- Week 2: Education / authority building
- Week 3: Social proof / results
- Week 4: Consideration / calls to action

Themes create a narrative arc across the month rather than a random sequence of posts.

## Phase 8: Trending Formats

The evergreen high-performing formats and the algorithm notes for each major platform are in `references/social-platform-specs.md`. Include at least 4 of these proven formats in the calendar:

- Listicle thread, This vs That, Day in the Life, Tutorial Reel, Hot Take, Before/After, Myth vs Reality, Fill in the Blank, POV, Reaction, Tutorial Carousel, Data Reveal

**Trend adaptation framework:**
1. Identify the trend format (audio, visual style, caption structure)
2. Find the brand angle (how does this connect to the brand's pillars?)
3. Adapt within 24-48 hours (speed is critical for trend content)
4. Add brand-specific value — don't just copy, add unique insight
5. Use the trend's audio or format markers appropriately
