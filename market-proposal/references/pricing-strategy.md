# Pricing Strategy Reference — Marketing Proposals

## Why Three Tiers Work (The Psychology)

Presenting three options exploits the "compromise effect": when given three choices, people instinctively avoid extremes and gravitate toward the middle option. This is why:
- The high tier makes the middle tier feel reasonable (price anchoring)
- The low tier makes the middle tier feel like an upgrade worth having
- Most clients (60-70%) choose the middle tier when properly structured

**How to build the anchor:** Price the top tier at 2x-2.5x the middle tier. If your target is a $5,000/month retainer, price the top at $10,000-$12,000 and the bottom at $2,500-$3,000.

## Tier Naming

Never use Bronze/Silver/Gold or Basic/Standard/Premium — these feel cheap and imply the middle tier is generic. Use aspirational names that describe the business outcome:

| Theme | Low | Middle | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth-focused | Grow | Accelerate | Dominate |
| Outcome-focused | Visibility | Momentum | Market Leader |
| Partnership-focused | Foundation | Partner | Strategic |
| B2B-focused | Starter | Growth | Enterprise |

## Pricing Models by Engagement Type

| Model | When to Use | Typical Range | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly Retainer | Ongoing services, relationship-based | $2,000-$25,000/month | Agencies with consistent deliverables |
| Project-Based | Defined scope, one-time deliverable | $5,000-$100,000/project | Websites, audits, campaigns |
| Performance-Based | Client wants risk-sharing | Base + % of revenue/leads | High-confidence verticals (e-commerce, lead gen) |
| Hybrid | Complex engagements | Base retainer + performance bonus | Mid-market and up |
| Hourly | Advisory, consulting, ad-hoc | $150-$500/hour | Strategic consulting, not execution |

## Price Anchoring Techniques

1. **Present highest tier first** — Always show prices in descending order (Dominate → Accelerate → Grow). The first number seen becomes the anchor.

2. **"Most Popular" / "Recommended" badge** — Place on the middle tier. This social proof signal makes clients feel the choice is validated by others.

3. **Show what they save** — If the high tier includes everything in the middle + extras, show the value of those extras: "Strategic tier includes full SEO + link building program (worth $3,500/month separately)."

4. **Tie every price to ROI** — Never present a number in isolation. Include the line: "At your average deal value of $[X], you only need [Y] new customers per month for this to pay for itself."

5. **Annual payment discount** — Offer 10-15% off for annual upfront payment. This improves your cash flow and increases client commitment.

## ROI Projection Formula

```
Current monthly revenue from marketing channel = (Traffic × Conversion Rate × Deal Value)

Projected improvement:
- SEO/Content: +20-40% traffic over 6 months (conservative)
- CRO/Landing Pages: +30-60% conversion rate improvement
- Email Marketing: +15-25% revenue from existing list
- Paid Ads: Target 3-5x ROAS minimum

Conservative monthly revenue impact = Δ Traffic × Δ Conversion Rate × Deal Value × 0.5 (confidence factor)

6-month projected return = Monthly impact × 6
ROI ratio = 6-month return ÷ 6-month investment cost
```

Use ranges, not point estimates. "We project $20,000-$45,000 in additional monthly revenue by month 6" is more credible than "$32,500 exactly."

## Terms and Conditions Essentials

Include in proposal appendix or as a separate document:

1. **Payment Terms:** Net 15 or Net 30, payment methods, late payment penalties (1.5%/month)
2. **Contract Duration:** Minimum commitment (typically 3-6 months), auto-renewal terms
3. **Cancellation Policy:** Required notice (30-60 days), exit process, work-in-progress handling
4. **Scope Changes:** Process for out-of-scope requests + rate for additional work
5. **Intellectual Property:** Who owns deliverables, license terms for tools/assets
6. **Confidentiality:** NDA terms, how client data is handled
7. **Liability Limitations:** Cap on liability, force majeure
8. **Third-Party Costs:** Client responsibility for ad spend, software, stock images
9. **Results Disclaimer:** Marketing results not guaranteed, past performance context

## Scope Creep Prevention

Define client responsibilities explicitly:
- Timely feedback within [X] business days (content approvals, asset delivery)
- Designated point of contact with decision-making authority
- Access to accounts, tools, and historical data within [X] days of signing
- Ad budget provided directly (not through agency)
- Review and approval of all assets before publication
