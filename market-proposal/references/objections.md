# Common Client Objections — Response Scripts

## "It's too expensive"

**Never lower the price first.** Reframe as investment:

"I understand — let's look at the math together. At your current deal value of $[X] and close rate of [X%], you need [X] additional qualified leads per month for this to pay for itself. Based on our work with similar companies, we typically generate [range] leads in the first 90 days. Does that change how you're thinking about the investment?"

If they still push: offer a smaller starting scope (Phase 1 only), not a discount. Discounting signals that you were overcharging to begin with.

## "We can do this in-house"

"That's a great option for some companies — here's what the math usually looks like. A marketing manager with this skillset costs $60,000-$80,000/year in salary, plus benefits, recruiting, management overhead, and tools. That's $90,000-$120,000/year fully loaded before they've shipped anything. Our engagement starts at $[X]/month and gives you immediate execution, no ramp time, and specialized expertise across [X] disciplines. The question isn't cost — it's speed to results. How quickly do you need to see impact?"

## "We tried an agency before and it didn't work"

"I'd like to understand what specifically didn't work — that will help me tell you honestly if we'd have the same problem. Was it the strategy, the execution, the communication, the fit? [Wait for answer] ... Based on what you're describing, the issue was [X]. Here's what we do differently: [specific differentiation]. I'd suggest starting with a 90-day pilot with clear success criteria so you can evaluate with data, not hope."

## "We need to think about it"

"Of course — what specifically would be most helpful to think through? I'd rather address questions directly than have you guessing at answers. What's the main thing holding you back?" (Wait for the real objection. "Need to think about it" is almost always a proxy for something else.)

If they genuinely need time: "Understood. Let's put a specific time on the calendar — can we talk [day] at [time]? I'll send a calendar invite."

## "Can you guarantee results?"

"Honest answer: no, and any agency that guarantees specific results is either lying or taking on risk that will cause them to cut corners. What I can do is share what we've achieved for similar companies [show case studies], explain exactly how we'll measure success, and structure our engagement so you have clear visibility into performance every month. We can also include a performance component — a base retainer plus a bonus tied to hitting specific KPIs — so our incentives are completely aligned with your results."

## "We're talking to other agencies"

"Good — you should be. I'd actually encourage you to ask each agency these three questions: [1] Can you show me 3 results for companies in my industry at my stage? [2] Who specifically will work on my account? [3] What does the first 30 days look like in detail? The answers will tell you a lot about who's actually going to execute vs. who's good at pitching. I'm confident in our answers to all three."

## "The timeline is too long"

"I understand the urgency — let me show you why a compressed timeline actually costs you more. If we skip the foundation phase, we're optimizing a broken system and wasting your ad spend. The first 30 days are what make months 3-6 possible. That said, here's what we can accelerate: [specific items]. We can have [specific quick wins] live within [shorter timeframe] while we build the foundation in parallel."

## "We don't have budget right now"

"I hear you. Two options: we can start smaller — our Foundation phase at $[X]/month gets the audit, strategy, and quick wins done in 60 days, then we expand once you see results. Or, if timing is the issue, we can schedule a kickoff date 30-60 days out and lock in current pricing now with a signed letter of intent. Which situation is closer to yours — is it a cash flow issue, a budget cycle issue, or genuine uncertainty about the ROI?"
