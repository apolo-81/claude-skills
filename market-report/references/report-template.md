# Marketing Report Reference: Scoring Rubric and Templates

## Scoring Rubric — Factor by Factor

### 1. Website & Conversion (Weight: 25%)

Score each factor 0-10, average, then scale to 0-100.

| Factor | 0-3 (Poor) | 4-6 (Average) | 7-9 (Good) | 10 (Excellent) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Page load speed | Over 5s or Core Web Vitals failing | 3-5s, some CWV issues | 2-3s, most CWV passing | Under 2s, all CWV pass |
| Mobile responsiveness | Broken on mobile | Functional but clunky | Good mobile experience | Optimized for mobile-first |
| Above-the-fold clarity | Unclear value prop | Somewhat clear | Clear value prop | Instantly clear, compelling |
| Primary CTA visibility | No clear CTA | CTA present but weak | Clear CTA | Prominent, compelling CTA |
| Landing page conversion rate | Under 1% | 1-2% | 2-4% | Over 4% |
| Trust signals | No reviews, badges, or social proof | Some trust signals | Good trust signals | Strong social proof throughout |
| Navigation and UX | Confusing or broken | Functional | Clean and intuitive | Excellent UX and flow |
| Checkout/signup flow | Multiple friction points | Some friction | Low friction | Frictionless |
| Analytics implementation | No tracking | Basic GA only | GA + goals configured | Full attribution setup |
| A/B testing | Never tested | Occasional tests | Regular testing | Ongoing systematic testing |

### 2. SEO & Organic (Weight: 20%)

| Factor | 0-3 | 4-6 | 7-9 | 10 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Title tag optimization | Missing or wrong | Exists but suboptimal | Good, keyword-focused | Perfect — compelling + keyword |
| Meta descriptions | Missing | Generic | Specific and relevant | Click-optimized |
| Heading hierarchy | Broken or missing | Present but unstructured | Logical structure | Optimized for keywords + users |
| Content depth | Thin (under 300 words) | Basic coverage | Good depth | Comprehensive, E-E-A-T strong |
| Internal linking | None | Some links | Good internal linking | Strategic topical clusters |
| Backlink profile | No backlinks | Few low-quality | Some quality links | Strong authority |
| Organic traffic trend | Declining | Flat | Growing slowly | Growing significantly |
| Keyword rankings | Not ranking for any targets | Few low-value rankings | Some mid-value rankings | Ranking well for core terms |
| Technical SEO health | Major issues | Some issues | Minor issues | Clean |
| Schema markup | None | Basic | Good | Comprehensive |

### 3. Content & Messaging (Weight: 15%)

| Factor | 0-3 | 4-6 | 7-9 | 10 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Value proposition clarity | Unclear or generic | Somewhat clear | Clear and specific | Immediately compelling |
| Target audience specificity | Too broad | Somewhat targeted | Well-defined audience | Laser-focused, resonant |
| Problem-first framing | Features-first | Mixed | Mostly problem-first | Strong problem narrative |

## Revenue Impact Calculation Templates

### Template 1: Traffic-Based Impact

```
Current state:
- Monthly organic sessions: [X]
- Organic conversion rate: [X%]
- Organic leads/month: [X]
- Lead-to-customer close rate: [X%]
- Average customer value (annual): $[X]

Target improvement:
- SEO improvements: +[X]% traffic increase (6-month estimate)
- CRO improvements: [X%] → [X%] conversion rate

Impact calculation:
- New traffic: [X] × (1 + [X%]) = [X] sessions
- New conversions: [X] × [X%] = [X] leads
- Incremental leads: [X] - [X current] = [X] additional leads
- Incremental revenue: [X leads] × [close rate] × [ACV] = $[X]/month
- Annual impact: $[X]/month × 12 = $[X]/year

Conservative estimate (50% confidence): $[X]/month
```

### Template 2: Conversion Rate Impact

```
Current state:
- Monthly website visitors: [X]
- Current conversion rate: [X%]
- Current customers/month: [X]
- Average order value / MRR: $[X]
- Current monthly revenue from this channel: $[X]

CRO improvement scenario:
- Target conversion rate: [X%] (based on industry benchmark: [X%])
- New customers/month: [X visitors] × [X%] = [X]
- Incremental customers: [X] - [X current] = [X] additional
- Monthly impact: [X additional] × $[AOV] = $[X]/month

6-month cumulative impact: $[X] × 6 = $[X]
12-month cumulative impact: $[X] × 12 = $[X]
```

### Template 3: Email Revenue Impact

```
Current state:
- Email list size: [X subscribers]
- Current email revenue attribution: $[X]/month
- Revenue per subscriber (RPS): $[X]/month

Improvement scenario:
- List growth from improved capture: +[X%] → [X new subscribers/month]
- Improved open rate: [X%] → [X%]
- Improved click rate: [X%] → [X%]
- Estimated revenue per subscriber improvement: [X%]

Impact:
- List growth impact (12 months): [X new subs] × $[RPS] × 6 months avg = $[X]
- Engagement improvement impact: [X] × [X% improvement] × [X] = $[X]/month
- Combined 6-month impact: $[X]
```

## Quick Win Identification Framework

Quick wins are changes that score HIGH on impact and LOW on effort. Use this matrix to prioritize:

| Action | Impact | Effort | Time to Result | Priority Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fix broken title tags | HIGH | Very Low (1-2 hrs) | Immediate | 10/10 |
| Add missing meta descriptions | HIGH | Low (2-4 hrs) | 1-2 weeks | 9/10 |
| Add CTA above the fold | HIGH | Low (1-2 hrs) | Immediate | 10/10 |
| Compress hero images | MEDIUM | Very Low (1 hr) | Immediate | 8/10 |
| Fix broken internal links | MEDIUM | Low (2-4 hrs) | Immediate | 8/10 |
| Add trust signals to homepage | HIGH | Medium (4-8 hrs) | Immediate | 8/10 |
| Set up email welcome sequence | HIGH | Medium (4-8 hrs) | Immediate | 8/10 |
| Install analytics/conversion tracking | HIGH | Low (2-4 hrs) | Immediate | 9/10 |
| Add alt text to all images | LOW | Low (2-4 hrs) | 2-4 weeks | 5/10 |
| Submit sitemap to GSC | MEDIUM | Very Low (30 min) | 2-4 weeks | 7/10 |

**Priority Score Formula:**
```
Priority = (Impact × 2 + Speed to Result) / Effort
```

Where: Impact (1-5), Speed to Result (1-5, higher = faster), Effort (1-5, higher = more effort)

## Report Narrative Templates

### Executive Summary Templates

**For a client in "Critical" range (0-39):**
```
[Company]'s marketing is currently operating below the threshold needed to support
growth targets. With an overall marketing score of [X/100], there are significant
structural gaps across [top 2-3 areas] that are limiting acquisition and creating
unnecessary revenue leakage.

The good news: the gaps are well-defined and fixable. Our analysis identifies
$[X,XXX]/month in recoverable revenue through [3-4 specific improvements]. The
path forward is clear, and the quick wins alone — implementable within the first
two weeks — are estimated to recover $[X,XXX]/month.

We recommend starting with [specific action], which addresses the highest-impact
gap with the least execution risk. A 90-day focused effort on these fundamentals
would put [Company] in a position to begin scaling acquisition with confidence.
```

**For a client in "Good" range (70-84):**
```
[Company] has built a solid marketing foundation. With an overall score of [X/100],
most core functions are working — the website converts at above-industry-average rates,
organic traffic is growing, and the email list is engaged.

The opportunity now is to move from solid to excellent. Our analysis identifies
$[X,XXX]/month in additional revenue within reach through [2-3 specific improvements]
— primarily in [top 2 areas]. These are optimizations to a working system, not
fixes to a broken one.

We recommend a focused quarter on [specific areas], with [specific first action]
as the highest-leverage starting point given current traffic and conversion data.
```

### Finding Framing Guide

**Frame every finding as opportunity, not failure:**

| Instead of... | Write... |
|---|---|
| "Your conversion rate is terrible at 1.2%" | "Your conversion rate of 1.2% is below the industry benchmark of 3.1% — a 1.9% gap that, closed, would generate [X additional customers/month]" |
| "You have no email list" | "Email hasn't been built yet — this represents one of the highest-ROI opportunities available, given that email typically generates $36 for every $1 spent" |
| "Your social media is inactive" | "Social presence is an untapped channel — [competitor] generates [X engagement/month] from similar content, suggesting significant audience appetite for this content type" |
| "Your site is slow" | "Page load time above 4 seconds is estimated to be costing [X%] of visitors before they see your offer — a fix with measurable and immediate revenue impact" |

## Competitive Comparison Scoring Guide

When comparing the client to competitors, score each factor consistently:

**Website Quality (1-10):**
- Design quality and modernity
- Page speed (test via PageSpeed Insights)
- Mobile experience
- Content depth and quality

**SEO Visibility (1-10):**
- Estimated organic traffic (use Semrush/Ahrefs free tools or estimation)
- Number of ranking keywords
- Domain authority signals
- Content volume

**Content Quality (1-10):**
- Posting frequency
- Content depth and E-E-A-T signals
- Engagement signals (comments, shares)
- Content variety

**Social Presence (1-10):**
- Follower count relative to industry
- Posting frequency
- Engagement rate
- Community quality
